# ZeptoPaws — Mobile Pet Grooming Opportunity

## Context

India’s pet ecosystem is growing rapidly, especially in Tier-1 cities where pet ownership, disposable income, and demand for premium care are highest.

As part of this case study, I explored whether **Zepto could enter the pet grooming space** through a **mobile grooming van model**, integrated into its existing ecosystem.

The goal was **not** to sell pet products directly, but to evaluate grooming as:
- A trust-led service
- A high-frequency use case
- A potential entry point into a high-LTV pet parent segment

---

## Problem Statement

Pet parents in Tier-1 cities face consistent challenges with grooming today:

- Mess left behind after at-home grooming
- Inconsistent groomer quality
- Difficulty booking reliable slots
- Low transparency around hygiene and product safety
- Emotional anxiety around how pets are treated

Existing options (salons, at-home groomers, independent vans) are **fragmented**, unreliable, or inconvenient.

The core question:
> Can Zepto create a **reliable, hygienic, trust-first mobile grooming experience** that pet parents would consistently choose?

---

## Research Approach

I conducted **end-to-end primary and secondary research**, including:

- **2 surveys** (18 + 16 respondents)
- **Prototype usability testing**
- **2 WhatsApp interviews**
- **1 detailed Zoom interview**
- Competitor analysis (Urban Company, HUFT, independent vans)
- Regulatory & operational feasibility review
- Market sizing (TAM / SAM / SOM for Tier-1 India)

---

## Key User Insights

Across pet parents (dogs primarily, cats secondary), patterns were very consistent:

1. **Trust matters more than price**
   - Groomer behaviour and bonding is critical
   - Parents avoid switching once trust is built

2. **Home grooming mess is a major pain**
   - Hair, water, smell remain even after cleaning
   - This strongly favours a van-based solution

3. **Booking reliability is broken**
   - Slot unavailability
   - Last-minute cancellations
   - Inconsistent groomers across sessions

4. **Mobile grooming vans are attractive — if hygiene & professionalism are guaranteed**
   - Users are open to vans when safety and cleanliness are clear

5. **Grooming is high-frequency**
   - Typically every 1–2 months
   - ₹8,000–15,000 annual spend per pet

---

## Market Sizing (Tier-1 India)

Using industry data and user-validated spend:

- **TAM:** ~₹3,700 crore (Tier-1 grooming market)
- **SAM:** ~₹2,500 crore (Zepto operational cities)
- **SOM (3–5 yrs):**
  - Conservative: ₹50 crore (2%)
  - Moderate: ₹125 crore (5%)
  - Aggressive: ₹250 crore (10%)

Even a small share is meaningful because grooming creates a **high-LTV pet parent base**.

---

## Where Zepto Can Win

Based on competitive gaps and user research:

1. **ZeptoPaws Grooming Vans**
   - Clean, fully equipped, hygienic
   - Solves the biggest pain: home mess

2. **Preferred Groomer Matching**
   - Builds trust and repeat usage
   - Reduces anxiety and switching

3. **Reliable App-Led Booking**
   - Predictable time slots
   - Clear availability
   - Zepto’s on-time trust advantage

4. **Pet-Safe Product Ecosystem (Secondary)**
   - Groomers use vetted products
   - Parents can repurchase via Zepto
   - Cross-sell emerges naturally post-trust

---

## Prototype & UX Learnings

From usability testing:

- Users preferred **hour-based slots** over vague time buckets
- Add-ons should be context-aware (avoid redundancy)
- Address input must support non-society homes
- Visual clarity and friendliness were positively received

---

## Key Takeaway

This case validated that **mobile pet grooming is a real, trust-led opportunity** in Tier-1 India.

Grooming should be treated as:
- A loyalty entry point
- A trust engine
- A high-frequency behaviour

With the right execution, ZeptoPaws could become a **category-defining, defensible service**, while unlocking long-term value across Zepto’s broader pet ecosystem.
